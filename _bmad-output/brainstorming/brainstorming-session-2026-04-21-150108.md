# Brainstorming: The Recursive Deployment Engine

**Date:** April 21, 2026
**Project Status:** Structural Design Phase (MVE Validation)
**Objective:** Transform unstructured human touchpoints into a **Living Digital Twin** of project execution and operational memory.

---

## 1. Session Discovery: Problem & Outcome

**The Challenge:** Existing CRMs and project tools are static graveyards. For high-stakes municipal deployments (NYC infrastructure), tribal knowledge and political nuances are lost between phases. This system builds an **Agentic Operating System** where every meeting, field note, and email feeds a model that proactively guides the strategist to avoid past failures and capitalize on proven "Value Creation" hooks.

**Success Metrics:**

- **The Learning Schema:** Programmatic definition of "True Justifiable Beliefs."
- **Temporal & Political Relevance:** A retrieval system surfacing insights based on **Phase** and **Stakeholder Incentives**.
- **Tactical Friction Alerts:** A real-time engine flagging risks based on historical patterns (e.g., "This specific sequence historically leads to a 3-month PPI delay").
- **Agentic Orchestration:** Specialized agents for **Hierarchy Mapping**, **Prioritization**, and **Learning Synthesis**.

---

## 2. The Deployment State Machine (The 7 Phases)

The system tracks the deployment through these explicit gates, filtering the "Memory Palace" through the current state:

1. **Pre-sale / Scoping** — Defining the "Power Graph" and initial outcome goals.
2. **Preparation** — Technical logistics: Lidar rollout, data offloading protocols, PPI compliance, and resource allocation.
3. **Integration / Data Collection** — Field execution: Digital Twin calibration and ground-truth gathering.
4. **User Training** — Adoption: ensuring field techs and department staff can extract platform value.
5. **Value Creation Phase** — The Proof: quantifying the business case (e.g., "Top 10 blocks for repair; saved $X and 6 months of labor").
6. **Preparing for Expansion** — The Bridge: identifying departmental champions and connecting them to the Budget Holder.
7. **Expansion** — The Scale: building the case for a 10x rollout and increased contract value.

---

## 3. Agentic Roles & Mandates

### A. The Cartographer (Hierarchy & People Agent)

- **Mandate:** Map the **Incentive Graph**. It tracks **Incentive Decay** (how a stakeholder's support shifts as the project moves from "cool tech" to "labor disruption").
- **Utility:** Understands that a "Champion" in Scoping may become a "Blocker" in Integration if their specific KPI is threatened.

### B. The Master Strategist (Prioritization Agent)

- **Mandate:** Monitor **Milestones**, **Timelines**, and **Revenue Generating Activities (RGA)**.
- **Utility:** Weighs directions on objective metrics. It flags if you are over-indexing on "Integration" while the "Expansion" window is closing.

### C. The Oracle (Learning Agent)

- **Mandate:** Synthesize **Justifiable Beliefs**.
- **Utility:** Distinguishes noise from transferable insight. A "Learning" is defined as a **justifiable change in belief** (e.g., "The department does not value efficiency; it values liability reduction").

---

## 4. Tactical Friction Alert & Context Injection

To provide daily utility, the system runs a continuous background loop:

**Input:** Recording / sentiment from steering committee or field notes.

**Processing:**

1. Agent identifies the current **Phase**.
2. Agent cross-references the **Learning Repo** for similar historical phases.
3. Agent checks the **People Agent** for stakeholder incentive alignment.

**Example Tactical Friction Alert:**

> ⚠️ **Friction Alert:** You are engaging a "Contractor Oversight" stakeholder. Historical data shows that emphasizing "Automated Detection" with this profile creates job-security anxiety. **Re-frame** the value as "Safety Compliance" to align with their primary incentive.

---

## 5. Technical Grounding & Reality Checks

### Temporal RAG (The Bottleneck)

Standard RAG is insufficient. We must implement **Phase-Gated Retrieval**. The system must prioritize a 2-year-old note from a **Value Creation** phase over a 2-week-old note from a **Scoping** phase when the user is currently at the **Expansion** gate.

### The "Not-To-Build" Line

- **Do Not Automate:** Final political negotiations or high-level relationship "vibes."
- **Do Automate:** Synthesis of transcripts, milestone tracking, and the daily **Phase-Aware Digest**.

### MVE (Minimum Viable Effort)

**The 11th-Call Test:** Use the 10 demo calls (Discovery through Training). The system must **predict the primary blocker for the 11th call** (Expansion). If the agent identifies that a specific stakeholder incentive from call #3 will block the contract in call #11, the architecture is validated.

---

## 6. The Learning Loop (Weekly Review)

Insights follow a validation path to prevent "hallucinated" strategies:

1. **Proposed Insight:** "Department X consistently pushes back on Lidar costs on Fridays."
2. **Manual Solidification:** The Strategist approves / edits the insight during the weekly review.
3. **Persistence:** Solidified insights are injected into the **Guideline Document** for all future deployments.

## Technique Selection

**Approach:** AI-Recommended Techniques
**Analysis Context:** Recursive Deployment Engine with focus on programmatically defining Justifiable Beliefs, populating multi-agent mandates, and pressure-testing against the 11th-call MVE.

**Recommended Techniques (in order):**

- **Phase 1 — First Principles Thinking** *(creative)* — Strip the Learning Schema and phase model to fundamental truths; produce atoms and invariants.
- **Phase 2 — Role Playing** *(collaborative)* — Embody the three agents (Cartographer / Master Strategist / Oracle) and stakeholder archetypes to generate alerts, incentive fields, and adoption frictions.
- **Phase 3 — Reverse Brainstorming / Anti-Solution** *(creative / wild)* — Invert: how to guarantee the system hallucinates, misfires the digest, or loses trust. Convert failures to design rules and MVE test cases.

**AI Rationale:** Foundation-first to anchor later ideation in a crisp schema; multi-perspective role work to populate the agent + incentive space divergently; inversion at the end to harden the system against its most plausible failure modes (explicitly named by the user: "hallucinated strategies" and the 11th-call test).

## Technique 1 — First Principles Thinking

### Idea Log

**[FP #1] Consequentialist Learning**
_Concept:_ A learning is an **action → outcome** claim evaluated against one or more named objectives, with a prescribed change graded by type (tip / guideline / process change) for next time.
_Novelty:_ Avoids the common "insight = vibe" trap; each learning is forced to name *what* changed, *what* it affected, and *what* should now change because of it.

**[FP #2] Objective as First-Class Prerequisite**
_Concept:_ "Benefit" and "detraction" are meaningless without an explicit, named objective. Therefore **Objective** is not prose inside the PRD; it is a structured entity attached to every deployment and every phase, and a learning row must reference at least one.
_Novelty:_ Promotes objectives to schema peers of Phase and Stakeholder. Most CRMs leave this implicit; this system requires it to generate any learning at all.

**[FP #3] Change Taxonomy (Tip / Guideline / Process Edit)**
_Concept:_ The prescribed output of a learning is graded: **Tip** (just-in-time nudge, ephemeral), **Guideline** (phase checklist addition, durable), **Process Edit** (changes the canonical deployment model itself, org-wide).
_Novelty:_ Each grade has different persistence, review cadence, and authority required to approve. Mixing them is how "learnings" turn into noise.

**[FP #4] Predictive Stance by Default**
_Concept:_ Every learning is phrased predictively — *"in contexts like C, expect O"* — never as hard causation. Causation is an **optional human promotion**: the Strategist can, during weekly review, upgrade a row from "predictive" to "causal" once evidence accumulates across deployments. The machine never self-promotes to causal.
_Novelty:_ Ships epistemic humility as a schema field, not just a norm. Most learning systems quietly drift to "X causes Y" and then calcify.

**[FP #5] Timeline-Woven Evidence (Trajectory, not Note)**
_Concept:_ The evidence unit for a learning is **not a single transcript chunk** — it is a **trajectory fragment**: an ordered sequence of events (e.g., demo on day 3 → steering-committee sentiment on day 5 → stakeholder-X objection on day 7) that together imply the belief.
_Novelty:_ Forces the schema to store evidence as *ordered references* across sources and time, not a flat tag cloud. This is what "temporal relevance" actually means in storage, not just in retrieval.

_Sub-atoms (First Principles decomposition):_

**[FP #5.a] Event as First-Class Entity**
_Concept:_ An **event** is its own persistent object — `(source, content_ref, actors, time-triple, type, provenance)` — independent of any trajectory that references it. The same event can appear in many trajectories.
_Novelty:_ Decouples the ingestion layer from the claim-making layer. Ingestion produces events; trajectory-building runs on top and is cheap to re-run.

**[FP #5.b] Time as a Triple, Not a Scalar**
_Concept:_ Every event carries three time values: `event_time` (when it actually happened), `ingestion_time` (when the system saw it), `validity_window` (when it is relevant for learning inference). Ordering, retrieval, and staleness care about different ones.
_Novelty:_ Prevents the subtle but fatal bug of sorting by ingestion and calling it a timeline. Makes "back-filled" a first-class condition (forwarded emails, meetings that summarize prior uncaptured conversations).

**[FP #5.c] Partial Order, Not Total Order**
_Concept:_ Events in a trajectory form a **partial order**. Concurrent events (two stakeholders writing the same morning; a demo and a side-bar sentiment in the same meeting) are represented as such. A rendering layer may linearize for UX; the underlying model must not.
_Novelty:_ Captures real concurrency without forcing false precedence. Protects causal reasoning from timezone-and-batching noise.

**[FP #5.d] Trajectory as Claim-Scoped Projection**
_Concept:_ A **trajectory** is a named projection of events **for the purpose of one claim** — not a calendar span and not a "phase timeline." Boundary lives with the claim; the same events can form different trajectories under different claims.
_Novelty:_ Kills the tempting shortcut of "phase = trajectory." Phase is context; the claim is the boundary.

**[FP #5.e] Multi-Trajectory Beliefs (and Cross-Deployment Reach)**
_Concept:_ A single learning may be supported by **multiple trajectories**, including trajectories spanning different deployments (especially for external-dynamic learnings from [FP #7]). The schema is `learning ↔ many trajectories ↔ many events`, with events potentially sourced from different customers.
_Novelty:_ Lets patterns accumulate across cities and accounts without forcing a fake single-rope trajectory.

**[FP #5.f] Silence as an Event Type**
_Concept:_ Missing expected activity is itself signal and is stored as a **pseudo-event** (`expected-but-absent`) — e.g., "stakeholder-B expected in follow-up; absent" or "steering committee cancelled without reschedule." Pseudo-events join the partial order and can participate in trajectories.
_Novelty:_ [FP #18] blocker detection depends on silence; the trajectory substrate must be able to hold absences, not only presences.

**[FP #5.g] Trajectory Closure Criterion**
_Concept:_ Every trajectory has an explicit **closure rule**: it is frozen when a named condition is met (phase end, solidification action, timeout without new relevant signal, or human override). Before closure, the trajectory is **provisional** and any learning resting on it is speculative; after closure, re-opening requires manual action and is logged.
_Novelty:_ Without closure, trajectories sprawl and learnings drift under them. Closure is what turns "current belief" into "settled evidence."

_Cross-atom implications:_
- **[FP #9] Proposals** inherit a natural lifecycle: a proposal is a claim whose trajectories are all still *provisional*. Promotion = all trajectories closed AND at least one new-deployment confirmation.
- **[FP #15] Pending queries** from multi-agent negotiation are typed events in the trajectory substrate; the user's answer is itself an event that closes a pending-query state.
- **[FP #17] Versioned Relationship Matrix** is itself a long-running trajectory over identity/role claims — same substrate, same closure semantics, applied to People.

_Pre-mortem hardening (18-month failure scenarios → design responses):_

Scenarios surfaced: (1) Cherry-Pick Trust Collapse, (2) Long-Phase Zombie Trajectory, (3) Back-Fill Silent Mutation, (4) PTO False-Blocker, (5) Cross-Customer Leak, (6) Correction Cascade Abandonment.

**[FP #5.h] Event Attestation + "Why These Events?" Trace**
_Concept:_ Every event carries **attestation** (observer identity, corroborating source references, confidence). Every rendered trajectory carries a mandatory **"Why these events?" trace** — the selection criterion in readable form plus an affordance to reveal eligible-but-not-selected events.
_Novelty:_ Makes projections *explainable by construction*. Kills the "this thing cherry-picks" trust collapse by showing the teammate the selection logic and the omissions at once.

**[FP #5.i] Pluggable Closure Rules (per Learning Class)**
_Concept:_ Closure is **typed, not universal**. A learning class picks its closure rule from: phase-end, signal-timeout, solidification-action, cross-deployment-confirmation, human-override. Proposals can be **provisionally promoted** once N-of-M trajectories close. A **zombie-trajectory monitor** surfaces open trajectories that haven't seen relevant signal for T days.
_Novelty:_ Prevents write-only-drawer syndrome in long municipal phases. Closure becomes a design parameter instead of a single global policy.

**[FP #5.j] Evidence Snapshot on Solidification**
_Concept:_ Solidifying a learning **snapshots its evidence set** (event refs + their time-triples as-of-solidification). Future reads render from the snapshot by default. Late-arriving events create a **re-examination task** rather than silently rewriting the justification. Learnings carry `last_justification_change_at`.
_Novelty:_ Makes "what we believe and why" stable enough to be cited. Back-filled events become a *review prompt*, not a ghost edit.

**[FP #5.k] Silence Calibration (per-Identity, OOO-Aware)**
_Concept:_ **Expected-reply-window** is a per-identity property on the relationship graph ([FP #17]), defaulted by role and refined per person. **OOO / calendar signals** are first-class events; during OOO windows, silence pseudo-events are suppressed or heavily down-weighted. Blocker alerts below a confidence floor go to the daily digest only, never to real-time ping.
_Novelty:_ This is where [FP #18] blocker detection actually lives in practice. Prevents the PTO false-blocker that would destroy a stakeholder relationship in one send.

**[FP #5.l] Tenancy-Aware Projection with Abstraction Guarantee**
_Concept:_ When a trajectory's events cross customer boundaries, cross-tenancy readers see **abstracted descriptors only** — role class, outcome class, phase, time-relative descriptors — never raw content, names, or identifying snippets. Abstraction is enforced **at the query layer**, not in the UI. Cross-deployment patterns that cannot survive abstraction are **privately scoped** and do not propagate.
_Novelty:_ Makes cross-deployment learning legally shippable. Prevents the most likely legal/compliance failure of a multi-customer product in this market.

**[FP #5.m] Identity-Keyed Correction Cascade**
_Concept:_ Actor identity is a **first-class versioned entity** (same substrate as [FP #17]). Corrections edit the identity; every event referencing it updates automatically. Correction UX is a three-panel pattern: **before / after / affected artifacts**, with one-click "approve and recompute." Downstream learnings whose evidence would change auto-spawn **re-examination tasks**.
_Novelty:_ Makes corrections cheap and therefore actually happen. Hardens [FP #16] extraction and [FP #17] versioned matrix over the long run instead of letting small errors compound.

**[FP #6] Falsifiability Requirement**
_Concept:_ A row is only admitted as a learning if it is phrased so a future deployment can **confirm or disconfirm** it. If the claim cannot be tested in the field, it is filed as **lore** (useful context) rather than a learning.
_Novelty:_ Creates a hard gate between *belief* and *insight*. Lore is still stored and searchable, but it cannot trigger proactive alerts — only learnings can.

**[FP #7] Observer Bifurcation (Internal vs External)**
_Concept:_ The system has two classes of observers. **Internal**: the Deployment Strategist and teammates on the enterprise account (their own process, effort, follow-up volume). **External**: the customer's stakeholders (Deputy Commissioner, Field Tech, Contractor Oversight, Budget Office). Learnings are tagged by which observer class they describe.
_Novelty:_ "The kickoff should be 1 week not 2 days" is **internal-process**; "DOT middle-management prioritizes pothole stats over TCO" is **external-dynamic**. These two classes probably need different review cadences and different retrieval paths.

**[FP #8] Learning Provenance: User-Proposed vs Agent-Proposed**
_Concept:_ Every learning carries a **provenance tag** on how it was born: (a) **User-proposed** via a scheduled "learning prompt" session (e.g., weekly review), or (b) **Agent-proposed** via multi-agent synthesis over patterns and metadata. Both paths then go through the same Solidification gate.
_Novelty:_ Provenance is a schema field, not a UX detail. It affects default confidence, the review path, and how the system should present the learning to a future teammate ("I noticed..." vs "Kenny solidified...").

**[FP #9] Third Bin: Proposals / Hypotheses**
_Concept:_ Beyond **lore** (untestable context) and **learnings** (predictive + falsifiable), there is a third bin: **proposals** — argued-from-signal changes that have not yet been tested, e.g., "Kickoff should be 1 week not 2 days because observed signal X suggests dividends in phase Y." A proposal is born with explicit **test criteria** attached and is promoted to a learning once it holds up in at least one future deployment.
_Novelty:_ Distinguishes "we believe this will work" from "we have evidence this works" at the schema level. Without this bin, the system either rejects speculative good ideas or dilutes the learning pool with untested ones.

**[FP #10] Quantitative-Signal Feature Surface**
_Concept:_ Agent-proposed learnings require the Oracle (and the "agent conference") to read **aggregate communication metadata** as features — follow-up counts, time-on-site, sentiment series, meeting density, Q&A topic drift — not only transcript text. The 2-day-kickoff learning was born from *volume of follow-up questions after short on-site time*, i.e., meta-signal.
_Novelty:_ Forces a data pipeline that emits structured metrics per phase, not just an embedding over notes. This is the boring-but-critical infra most "AI CRMs" skip.

**[FP #11] Knowledge-Relay as End Purpose**
_Concept:_ The real end consumer of a learning is **a future teammate on a new deployment**, not the machine and not you. Every learning row and every proactive alert must pass the test: *"Would a teammate six months from now, with no context on the source deployment, read this and act correctly?"*
_Novelty:_ Promotes **teammate legibility** to a hard acceptance criterion. It shapes the rendering, the required context fields (deployment, phase, stakeholder role, trajectory refs), and the solidification rubric.

_Sub-atoms (Reverse Engineering from a teammate moment-of-value — "Jamie, month 3 of Chicago Phase 2, steering committee tomorrow, opens For You"):_

**[FP #11.a] Teammate Legibility Standard**
_Concept:_ Every learning conforms to a fixed schema before it can be surfaced: **claim**, **context** (source deployment + phase + stakeholder role class), **confidence**, **trajectory summary** (or abstracted equivalent under [FP #5.l]), **suggested action**. Free-form learnings are rejected from the surfacing layer.
_Novelty:_ Makes surfaced content **parseable by a reader with zero source context**. The teammate-six-months-later test becomes a schema check, not a vibe check.

**[FP #11.b] "Why This For You Now" Trace**
_Concept:_ Every proactively surfaced item carries a one-sentence grounding in the **reader's current state** — their deployment, phase, upcoming stakeholder interactions, and open action items. Not "this is a good learning"; "this is a good learning *for Jamie, in Chicago Phase 2, before her 2pm steering committee*."
_Novelty:_ Replaces generic recommendations with **contextual grounding**. Agent trust is built by the grounding being correct, not by the recommendation being right.

**[FP #11.c] Rejection as Signal (Teammate Feedback Loop)**
_Concept:_ A reader's "not applicable to me" is a **typed event** on the learning with optional reason class (context-mismatch, stale, disagree-with-reasoning, already-handled). Rejection patterns across teammates degrade default confidence and can trigger re-examination.
_Novelty:_ Turns distributed skepticism into **calibration**. Prevents the "everyone ignores the banner" failure mode of internal recommender systems.

**[FP #11.d] Action-Linked Outcome Tracking**
_Concept:_ When a teammate acts on a surfaced learning, the **resulting event** (meeting outcome, blocker resolution, expansion conversation outcome) attaches back to the learning's trajectory substrate as a **confirming or disconfirming event**. Over time, the learning's real-world track record is visible on the row itself.
_Novelty:_ Closes the loop between surfacing and validation. Learnings that never produce follow-through, or whose follow-through disconfirms them, surface as review candidates automatically.

**[FP #11.e] Context Similarity Scoring**
_Concept:_ Proactive surfacing requires a **scoring function** between reader context and source context (deployment, phase, stakeholder role class, outcome class). Low similarity suppresses surfacing entirely or downgrades it to the daily digest.
_Novelty:_ Prevents the generic-RAG failure where text similarity drives surfacing while situational similarity is low (NYC DOT pattern shown to a Vermont town strategist).

**[FP #12] True Multi-Agent Negotiation with Human Escalation**
_Concept:_ Sub-agents (Cartographer, Master Strategist, Oracle, and peers) hold defined roles and **actually negotiate** — exchange positions, argue priority, and converge. During negotiation, any agent can **query the user** for a missing judgment, and the user can also **weigh in unprompted**. The output is a committed state, not an echo.
_Novelty:_ "Agent conference" becomes a real protocol with escalation primitives, not a UI flourish. This is the central mechanism; the schema must support **pending user queries** and **reopened negotiations** when the user weighs in.

_Sub-atoms (First Principles decomposition of the negotiation protocol):_

**[FP #12.a] Negotiation Trigger (Entry Condition)**
_Concept:_ A negotiation opens only when (a) signal from one agent crosses a pre-declared threshold, (b) two or more sub-agents hold **conflicting positions** on overlapping state, or (c) the user explicitly requests one. Routine observations are committed by the originating agent without ceremony.
_Novelty:_ Keeps the overhead of "negotiation" out of high-volume extraction work. The substrate updates continuously; negotiation is the exception, not the rule.

**[FP #12.b] Role-Typed Positions as Structured Documents**
_Concept:_ Each participating sub-agent contributes a **typed position document**: Cartographer brings identity/relation proposals; Oracle brings candidate learnings or prior-pattern matches; Master Strategist brings priority scores under [FP #13]; Blocker Agent brings candidate blockers with confidence. Free-text positions are rejected.
_Novelty:_ Forces agent interoperability at the **data-shape layer**, not the prose layer. Enables deterministic merging and independent evolution of each agent's internals.

**[FP #12.c] Termination Conditions (Four Types)**
_Concept:_ A negotiation ends when **one of four** conditions is met: (1) positions reconcile into a joint proposal, (2) a human query is escalated ([FP #15]) and the negotiation pauses, (3) a deadlock detector fires (cycle of mutual rejection), or (4) a TTL timeout elapses. Each termination type emits a different artifact.
_Novelty:_ Makes every negotiation bounded and observable. No negotiation runs indefinitely; every one produces something durable and typed.

**[FP #12.d] Commit and Reject as Durable Artifacts**
_Concept:_ A committed negotiation emits a durable state change (new learning, new action item, new identity version, a relation diff). A **rejected** negotiation emits a **rejection record** — what was considered, positions held, why reconciliation failed. Rejection records are searchable and feed system-level evaluation.
_Novelty:_ Treats failed negotiations as **first-class memory**, not exhaust. Patterns of repeated rejection are signal about agent calibration, not noise to suppress.

**[FP #12.e] Silent-Consensus Path**
_Concept:_ If only one sub-agent holds a position on a state and no contestant emerges within a short window, the state **commits without a full negotiation**. Only active disagreement triggers full ceremony.
_Novelty:_ Avoids the "everything is a meeting" failure mode. Most of the product's daily state-updates commit silently and fast; full negotiation is reserved for real conflict.

**[FP #12.f] Interruption and Re-Entry**
_Concept:_ A user interjection mid-negotiation is captured as a **typed event** (new position, override, deferral), pauses the current state, and **re-enters** the negotiation with the user's input as a new position. Prior positions are preserved in the negotiation record.
_Novelty:_ Makes human involvement **additive, not destructive**. Interventions are traceable and reversible in the audit log; the negotiation remembers both the pre- and post-intervention paths.

**[FP #13] Dual-Objective Stack (Phase-Local + North-Star-Global)**
_Concept:_ Every negotiation evaluates candidate moves against **two objectives simultaneously**: the current **phase objective** (e.g., Value Creation = prove ROI; Integration = ground-truth quality; Scoping = define power graph) *and* the ever-present **North Star** (scale this account + land more of the company's product lines). Learnings and action items are scored on both axes, never one.
_Novelty:_ Promotes the growth/expansion objective from marketing language to a **first-class scoring dimension** that the Master Strategist always considers, even during deep phase execution.

_Sub-atoms (First Principles decomposition of dual-objective scoring):_

**[FP #13.a] Phase Objective as Gate, North Star as Compound**
_Concept:_ Phase objectives are **gating** (binary or thresholded: must be satisfied to progress), while North Star contributions are **compounding** (continuous, additive over time). They live on different scales and must not be collapsed into a single number.
_Novelty:_ Kills the naive weighted-sum approach. Makes explicit that "is this phase on track?" and "is this growing the account?" are different questions with different decision semantics.

**[FP #13.b] Pareto Frontier First, Priority Second**
_Concept:_ Candidate actions are plotted on a **2D frontier** (phase-progress × north-star-impact). The Master Strategist identifies the frontier first, then collapses to a single priority only when needed for the action queue. Dominated candidates are filtered before ranking.
_Novelty:_ Preserves the trade-off conversation inside the schema. The Strategist can ask "what's on the frontier?" and see the actual alternatives, not a collapsed ranking.

**[FP #13.c] Trade-off as Negotiation-Escalated, Not Auto-Resolved**
_Concept:_ When two candidates sit at opposite ends of the frontier (one strong on phase, one strong on North Star), the system **escalates** to a multi-agent negotiation ([FP #12]) or to the user — it does not silently apply a weighting.
_Novelty:_ Preserves strategic judgment as a human+agent act, not a hidden config value. Prevents the subtle harm of "the product made a trade-off I didn't know I'd authorized."

**[FP #13.d] Time Horizon as a Scoring Dimension**
_Concept:_ North Star contribution always carries a **time horizon label** (this-quarter, this-year, over-account-lifetime). Scoring buckets are horizon-segregated; a near-term phase win never competes directly with a multi-year North Star move on a shared scale.
_Novelty:_ Prevents short-term bias. Keeps "long game" visible in the queue without drowning "this week."

**[FP #13.e] Per-Customer Scoring Calibration**
_Concept:_ The weight between phase-objective urgency and North Star impact is **tenant-configurable per customer**, and may itself evolve into a learning over time. A procurement-heavy account may tilt toward phase-compliance; a fast-expanding account may tilt toward North Star contribution.
_Novelty:_ Encodes that "the right priority" is **customer-specific by design, not by bug**. The same candidate action can legitimately rank differently in NYC vs. a smaller city.

**[FP #14] Action Items as a First-Class Output Type**
_Concept:_ Each agent negotiation produces **two artifact classes**: (1) candidate **learnings / proposals** for the long-lived knowledge store, and (2) a **prioritized action queue** for the short term (what to do this week, and in what order). The action queue persists across negotiations and is itself input to the next negotiation.
_Novelty:_ Separates *memory* (learnings) from *attention* (action items). Most agentic systems collapse these into "recommendations"; here they are distinct bins with distinct lifecycles and distinct UI surfaces (the Strategist's dashboard is literally the action queue).

_Sub-atoms (Reverse Engineering from Monday 8am — "Kenny opens the product; the queue shows seven items"):_

**[FP #14.a] Action Item Schema**
_Concept:_ Every action item carries a fixed schema: **what** to do, **why-now** grounding, **priority** (frontier position under [FP #13]), **effort estimate**, **expected outcome**, **linked events/stakeholders**, **owner**, **completion criteria**, **age**. Items not meeting the schema are rejected at enqueue.
_Novelty:_ Makes the queue **audit-able and portable**. Items can be handed off between teammates or reviewed retrospectively without losing context.

**[FP #14.b] Staleness and Decay Rules**
_Concept:_ Action items age. Stale items (past their relevance window without progress) are **re-promoted with updated context**, **retired with a recorded reason**, or **escalated for explicit user decision**. No silent disappearance.
_Novelty:_ Prevents the action-queue-as-graveyard failure that kills every task system. Decay is typed behavior with audit, not neglect.

**[FP #14.c] Completion → Evidence Loop**
_Concept:_ Marking an item complete spawns a **completion event** that attaches back to the trajectories and learnings that informed it. If the action was suggested by a learning, the outcome confirms or disconfirms that learning's predictive claim.
_Novelty:_ Closes the loop between action and memory. The action queue is not just an output of the schema — it is an **input** back into it, producing the self-calibrating behavior the north-star architecture needs.

**[FP #14.d] Queue Saturation Gate**
_Concept:_ The Master Strategist holds a **soft cap** on the queue. Above the cap, new candidates must **compete** with existing items on priority; low-priority incumbents are demoted or retired rather than letting the queue grow indefinitely.
_Novelty:_ Forces honest prioritization at the source. Protects the psychological contract: "what's on the queue today is what matters today" — not a backlog of good-ideas-that-never-happen.

**[FP #14.e] Typed Owners (Self / Teammate / Tracked)**
_Concept:_ Each item has a typed owner: **Self** (primary Strategist), **Teammate** (delegated, with a lightweight delegation event), or **Tracked** (watched for external progress; no Strategist action needed, but state transitions are detected and surfaced). Items can move between types.
_Novelty:_ Makes **"things I'm waiting on" first-class** alongside "things to do." The Tracked class is where most municipal deployment work actually lives and is invisible in conventional task systems.

**[FP #14.f] Rejection-with-Reason as Event**
_Concept:_ Declining an action is a **typed event** with a required reason class: *not-applicable*, *wrong-time*, *disagree-with-reasoning*, *already-handled*, *out-of-scope*. Rejection patterns feed back into agent calibration and can trigger re-examination of the source learning.
_Novelty:_ Turns dismissal into signal. "I disagreed with the reasoning" is gold for tightening an agent's prompt, logic, or confidence thresholds — captured automatically, not via a survey.

**[FP #15] Human-in-the-Loop Escalation Primitive**
_Concept:_ The multi-agent protocol natively supports a **pending-query** state: an agent can pause its position on the condition of a one-question answer from the Strategist. Unanswered queries are visible in the dashboard; an answer (or user-initiated override) **reopens** the negotiation and updates downstream action items.
_Novelty:_ Makes the human an **explicit node in the graph**, not an afterthought. Prevents "agent confidently wrong" failures that come from forcing full autonomy on ambiguous cases.

**[FP #16] Passive Continuous Entity & Relation Extraction**
_Concept:_ Mapping the customer footprint is the **agent's job**, not the user's. Every ingested artifact (email threads with sender + CCs + org signatures, voice notes, meeting transcripts, shared docs, calendar invites) is auto-parsed into **entities** (people, committees, agencies, vendors, contractors) and **relations** (reports-to, sits-on, advises, blocks, champions, co-mentions). The user corrects; they never build from scratch.
_Novelty:_ The org chart is not an input — it is an **inferred output** that updates with every message. Drops the biggest data-entry tax that kills CRM adoption.

_Sub-atoms (Pre-mortem hardening for passive extraction — 18-month failure scenarios: "three Johns" ambiguity, over/under-extraction, role-inference drift, vendor/agency confusion, corrections don't stick, inflated relation strength):_

**[FP #16.a] Confidence-Graded Extraction with Reason Trace**
_Concept:_ Every extracted entity and relation carries a **confidence score** and a **reason trace** (the source references, the heuristic or model that produced it, corroborating signals). Low-confidence extractions render as **drafts**, not facts, and are segregated in UI.
_Novelty:_ Makes the Cartographer's output **inspectable by default**. Prevents confident-wrong extractions from silently hardening into "truth" the user never reviewed.

**[FP #16.b] Identity Resolution as a Dedicated Agent Loop**
_Concept:_ Identity resolution is its own agent task with three modes: **auto-resolve** (high-confidence matches), **queue for user** (medium-confidence; a disambiguation question is surfaced in the dashboard), **keep split as distinct** (low-confidence; stays split until corroborating signal arrives). A visible backlog prevents silent merges.
_Novelty:_ Treats the "three Johns" problem as a **dedicated workflow**, not an NLP side-effect. Disambiguation becomes a user-visible affordance with audit.

**[FP #16.c] Relation Strength Ladder (with Typed Relations)**
_Concept:_ Relations are scored on a **strength ladder** by source signal: org-signature mention (weak), single CC (very weak), repeated co-occurrence (medium), direct to:from (strong), explicit user assertion (authoritative). Untyped "connected to" is **rejected as signal**; relations must be typed (reports-to, advises, approves-budget-for, vendor-of, sits-on-committee, absent-from-expected-meetings, etc.).
_Novelty:_ Turns relationships into **structured data with defensible strength semantics**. Kills the "everyone is connected to everyone" noise that destroys graph-based CRMs.

**[FP #16.d] Correction-as-Lock (Sticky Ground Truth)**
_Concept:_ User-applied corrections become **sticky ground truth** on the extracted entity/relation. Future extractions defer to them unless **contradicted with high-confidence evidence**, which opens a **re-evaluation task** — never a silent override.
_Novelty:_ Prevents the "I corrected this yesterday and it's wrong again today" failure that destroys trust in any continuous-extraction system. Pairs with [FP #5.m] identity-keyed cascade.

**[FP #16.e] Extraction Review Digest**
_Concept:_ The system produces a **periodic extraction digest** (daily or weekly) that surfaces recent extractions, flags low-confidence items, highlights role inferences that changed, and prompts for user review on the highest-impact new entities and relations. Review actions feed back into [FP #16.d] corrections.
_Novelty:_ Makes silent drift **visible by design**. The user doesn't have to discover wrong extractions by accident; they get a structured surface for catching them regularly.

**[FP #16.f] Role Change Detection**
_Concept:_ Identity records carry **role versions**; when extraction signals a role shift (title change in a signature, vocabulary shift, new committee membership, promotion announcement), a **role-change event** is emitted and routed through negotiation ([FP #12]) for confirmation. Role history is queryable ("what did we believe John's role was in phase 2 vs. phase 5?").
_Novelty:_ Makes **incentive-decay detection mechanically possible**. Without role versioning, the relationship graph silently calcifies and the Cartographer stops being useful after month 6 of an account.

**[FP #17] Living, Versioned Relationship Matrix**
_Concept:_ The relationship graph **recalibrates** on new signal and keeps a **versioned history** — you can query "what did we believe about this stakeholder in phase 3 vs phase 5?" Incentive decay is detected by **diffs** across versions. Versioning also carries over to permissions and compliance (user-group changes, access rights).
_Novelty:_ Combines CRM functionality with **git-like provenance** for relationships. Makes "this stakeholder flipped" a machine-detectable event, not a human post-mortem.

**[FP #18] Blocker as Cross-Modal Discernment**
_Concept:_ Blockers are **not a field the user fills** — they are continuously inferred from multi-modal signal across time: tone shift in voice notes, unanswered email threads, canceled / re-scheduled meetings, vocabulary drift ("let's pause" → "let's re-evaluate"), absent stakeholders. A candidate blocker is raised into negotiation the moment its signal cluster crosses threshold.
_Novelty:_ Blocker detection is a **standing agent task**, not a report the user runs. It is a cross-modal classifier, not a keyword search.

**[FP #19] Senior-Strategist Mindset as Agent Design Constraint**
_Concept:_ Every sub-agent is prompted, tooled, and evaluated to act with the judgment, compression, and vocabulary of a **senior deployment strategist** — someone who has scaled municipal accounts before and knows what matters at each phase. Not a transcription bot, not a CRM auto-tagger.
_Novelty:_ Treats agent persona as a **cross-cutting quality bar**, enforced via evals (e.g., "would a senior strategist raise this as the #1 action right now?"). This is how the "senior" felt quality is protected as the roster of sub-agents grows.

**[FP #B — Typology Agreed]**
_Concept:_ Learning targets are the four-slot typology: **People / Process / Pitch / Strategy-Growth**. Tools/Platform and Economics/Contracting collapse into specialized sub-tags inside **Process** and **Strategy** rather than earning their own slot.
_Novelty:_ **People** is the *most active* surface because [FP #16] and [FP #17] continuously feed it; it is not a sidecar taxonomy, it is an always-running engine.

## Technique 2 — Role Playing

**Purpose:** Embody the sub-agents and stakeholder archetypes on top of the Phase 1 substrate. Generate **alerts, action-item templates, incentive-field shapes, adoption frictions, and prompt-level constraints** by running real scenarios from multiple viewpoints. This is divergence time; Phase 1 sub-atoms are the constraints the scenarios must honor, not the output to be produced.

**Rotation plan:**

1. **The Cartographer** (Hierarchy & People Agent) — first; your earlier notes gave the richest raw material here.
2. **The Oracle** (Learning Agent) — next; it is the main consumer of [FP #5] and the producer of most proactive surfacings.
3. **The Master Strategist** (Prioritization Agent) — third; pressure-tests [FP #13] and [FP #14].
4. **The Blocker Agent** (implied by [FP #18] + [FP #5.k]) — fourth.
5. **Stakeholder archetypes (flip):** Deputy Commissioner / Budget Holder, Field Tech, Contractor Oversight, Political Liaison, Champion-turning-Blocker, future Teammate — to generate frictions and adoption realities.

### Phase 2 — Role #1: The Cartographer

**Scene 1 — "The forwarded thread (routine reschedule)"**

*Day 47 of the NYC deployment. Kenny forwards a 5-person email in which Maria moves the steering committee date and notes she'll be tied up, John will run it, Tamika is out until the 22nd, and the vendor rep should await final dates.*

**Design principles surfaced (user-supplied reaction; Cartographer design corrected):**

**[RP-CART #1] Entity Shape — Person × Org × Role × AdvocacyScore(t)**
_Concept:_ Every entity in the Cartographer graph is a **Person within an Org, with a Role, and an AdvocacyScore that evolves over time** — advocacy measuring how strongly this person promotes/supports our deployment and our expansion. All four fields are always present; unknown values are explicitly held.
_Novelty:_ AdvocacyScore(t) becomes the scalar that incentive-decay acts on; it is the single axis the Cartographer cares most about maintaining well.

**[RP-CART #2] User as Active Context Contributor (not only Corrector)**
_Concept:_ Users have three actions on an entity: (a) **fill** an unknown field, (b) **correct** an existing field, (c) **defer** ("I don't know yet — re-ask when more context arrives"). Proactive filling is a first-class path, not a nudge.
_Novelty:_ Extends [FP #16.d] sticky ground truth from a correction primitive to a **contribution primitive**. Users build the graph collaboratively with the agent.

**[RP-CART #3] "I Don't Know Yet" as a Durable Field State**
_Concept:_ Entity fields have three value states: **known**, **unknown-but-resolvable-by-signal** (Cartographer keeps trying), **user-deferred** (explicitly paused until context arrives). Deferred fields are excluded from confidence scoring and from matrix weighting, and surface in the digest when new signal touches them.
_Novelty:_ Makes "I'm not sure" a **typed state**, not a gap. Prevents the product from pestering the user on things they already decided to defer.

**[RP-CART #4] Context-Depth Weighting in the Stakeholder Matrix**
_Concept:_ Entities with less accumulated context get **lower default weight** in the stakeholder matrix. Rendering visually indicates "who we know well" vs "who we barely know." High-AdvocacyScore entities with **low context** are flagged for Strategist investigation (potentially our most important unknown).
_Novelty:_ Turns the matrix into a **legibility surface**, not a completeness illusion. Also creates a natural "investigation queue" for human time allocation.

**[RP-CART #5] Mission-Relevance Triage (pre-extraction gate)**
_Concept:_ Before rich extraction runs, a **relevance grader** classifies the incoming artifact: (a) **routine / log-only** (participants + metadata stored, no extraction), (b) **potentially relevant** (queue for thread-level analysis), (c) **high-relevance** (rich extraction + possible negotiation trigger). Most communications are routine.
_Novelty:_ Saves ceremony for signal. Without this, the Cartographer becomes noisy and expensive and the user stops trusting its outputs because most of them are about nothing.

**[RP-CART #6] Thread (and Session) as the Extraction Unit**
_Concept:_ The Cartographer's primary unit of extraction is **not a single email** — it is a **thread** (emails in a conversation), a **meeting cluster**, or a **voice-note session**. Aggregated communication accumulates the context that makes a relation or a role-change claim defensible.
_Novelty:_ Aligns [FP #5.d] trajectory-as-projection with the ingestion side: projections are also how ingestion operates, not just how claims are made.

**Corrected Scene 1 interpretation (what the Cartographer should actually do on this routine email):**

- Log participants on the thread (no new entities; all known at day 47).
- Record the reschedule as a **low-salience event** (`meeting_rescheduled`, phase-attached).
- Capture absence signals ("Maria tied up," "Tamika out until 22nd") as **pseudo-events** under [FP #5.f], feeding silence-calibration [FP #5.k] but **not** triggering role-change events.
- Do **not** trigger negotiation. Do **not** open disambiguation queries on a graph that already knows these five people.
- **If** this email were one of three recent messages showing Maria stepping back from SCs, the *accumulated thread-context* would raise a role-change candidate — and **only then** would the Cartographer enter negotiation with the Oracle and Master Strategist.

**[RP-CART #7] Full-Name Requirement + Web-Aware Disambiguation Agent**
_Concept:_ Every person entity carries **first + last name** at minimum. When duplicates exist ("three Johns") or a new entity lacks context, a **web-aware sub-agent** cross-references public sources — LinkedIn, government registries, press releases, committee rosters, news archives — to add identifying and role context. Findings enter with **source-attribution** and a confidence score, never as anonymous "facts."
_Novelty:_ Promotes **external cross-referencing** to a first-class Cartographer capability, not a stretch goal. Every public signal that disambiguates or enriches an identity reduces the user's validation burden.

**[RP-CART #8] Symbiotic Posture — Surface, Don't Authorize**
_Concept:_ The agent's default posture is to **surface issues for the human**, not to be the ultimate authority. A per-operation **confidence threshold** gates auto-commit: above threshold, the Cartographer commits silently; below threshold, the proposed change enters a **User Validation Queue** that the Strategist combs regularly. The philosophy is explicit: the agent is a **digital-twin brain** that helps a human track long-standing deployments, not a decision-maker that replaces one.
_Novelty:_ Encodes that **over-confident agents destroy strategist trust** and that under-confident agents destroy strategist productivity. Makes "what confidence do I have on this?" a question the system answers every time, routed either to silent commit or to a visible queue.

**[ENG #1] Memory State as Anti-Hallucination / Anti-Drift Substrate**
_Concept:_ The system's durability comes from a **canonical memory state** — the event log, the identity graph, the solidified-learning library, the action queue — that is the *single source of truth*. Agents never generate claims ex nihilo; every surfaced fact must resolve to a specific entry in the canonical store with a traceable lineage. This is how the system stays "long-standing": the memory persists across LLM model changes, agent restarts, strategist turnover, and multi-month deployments.
_Anti-hallucination contract:_ every agent output carries a **citation envelope** — which events, which identity nodes, which solidified learnings, which phase-state it was grounded in. Outputs without a citation envelope are rejected at the agent boundary (not just in UI). The strategist can always click back to primary evidence.
_Anti-drift controls:_ (a) Identity-keyed lookups hit the canonical graph, never agent in-context memory. (b) Periodic **consistency audits** compare agent-surfaced facts against the canonical store; disagreements raise alerts and enter the User Validation Queue, never silently overwrite. (c) **Retrieval-bound generation** — agents cannot emit claims about identities, events, or learnings that aren't in the store; they can only propose additions (which route to normal tier rules per [FP #16.c] and [FP #5.h]). (d) Structured outputs validated by schema; schema violations fail closed.
_Novelty:_ Encodes that the product's moat is the memory, not the model. LLMs are swappable; the graph / event log / learning library is not. Also operationalizes [FP #11] (knowledge-relay) as a non-functional requirement: future teammates inherit a verifiable store, not an agent's opinion of the deployment.

**[ENG #2] Test Coverage Mandate — 100% Uptime Target**
_Concept:_ Every code path ships with tests. Uptime target is 100%, not best-effort. Applies to both deterministic code (event store, identity graph writes, phase-state transitions, retrieval queries, closure rules, schema validation) and the agent boundary (structured-output contracts, tier gating, citation-envelope enforcement, negotiation termination).
_Practical surface:_ (a) **Unit tests** on all deterministic logic; no merge without them. (b) **Schema contract tests** on every agent's I/O — if an LLM returns malformed output, the test catches the contract, not production. (c) **Fallback-path tests** for every LLM call: what happens when the model fails schema, times out, or returns low-confidence output. (d) **Replay tests** — seed the system with a known event sequence, assert that the graph, learnings, and digests produced are exactly what was previously captured; catches drift in agent behavior across model updates. (e) **Canonical-store invariant tests** — e.g., "no identity ever has two concurrent active role versions," "every action item resolves to at least one event," "no solidified learning exists without an evidence snapshot per [FP #5.i]."
_Novelty:_ Makes testing a product feature of a memory system, not an afterthought of a chat system. Replay tests in particular act as a regression net for LLM behavior drift — without them, each model upgrade silently re-interprets the past.

**[ENG #3] Adaptive Agent Logic via Continuous Real-World Tuning**
_Concept:_ Every agent's decision logic — tier thresholds, confidence floors, ranker weights, silence calibration, phase-transition detectors, semantic triggers, latency budgets — must be **tunable over time based on real-world feedback**. Agents are not statically configured; their parameters move as disposition events accumulate ([RP-ORACLE #10]), as strategist overrides pile up ([RP-ORACLE #2]), as the cross-deployment corpus grows. The system gets smarter the longer it runs against a deployment.
_Practical surface:_ (a) Every agent parameter has a **calibration history** — prior value, new value, triggering events, timestamp. (b) Calibration changes are driven only by canonical events (dispositions, overrides, promotions, false-positive marks) — never by silent background drift. (c) Calibration changes are themselves events in the log with attestation per [FP #5.h] — the system can always explain why it behaves differently today than three months ago. (d) A strategist-facing **calibration review** surface lets the human inspect and, if needed, roll back a tuning change. (e) Replay tests per [ENG #2] pin calibration *behavior* at known input/output pairs so tuning is traceable regression, not surprise behavior change.
_Novelty:_ Frames agent learning as a first-class, audited, human-reviewable process — not a hidden ML feedback loop. Keeps the durability contract of [ENG #1] (canonical memory) while allowing the agents to get sharper with real use. Matches the product posture: the system adapts, but never silently; every change has a reason traceable in the log.

**[SCOPE #2] First-Deployment-First — No Premature Cross-Corpus Engineering**
_Concept:_ V1 is designed and built to work **excellently for a single deployment**. The first real deployment is the blueprint; subsequent deployments grow on top of it. Cross-deployment machinery (corpus-confidence tiering, across-corpus promotion, cross-customer abstraction rules, multi-deployment statistical calibration) is **architected into the data model** but **not operationally engineered** before N≥3 real deployments exist. For V1: the schema supports it, the UI surfaces it as "coming online as more deployments run," and the logic treats the single-deployment corpus as the primary store.
_Demo vs. real data posture:_ The database will be **seeded** with representative data to demonstrate the logic (morning digests, alerts, evening synthesis, identity graph visualization). **Real calibration** — the kind that tunes thresholds, ranker weights, silence calibration, phase-transition detectors — **only begins when a real deployment is running.** Seeded data proves the system works; it does not substitute for real-world tuning, and the system does not pretend it does.
_Implication for prior atoms:_ [RP-ORACLE #3] corpus-confidence remains a first-class field but carries "N=1 seeded" or "N=1 real" explicitly until N grows. [FP #5.l] tenancy-aware projection is built as a capability but is a no-op until a second real deployment lands. [ENG #3] adaptive tuning runs against real deployments only; seeded-data runs do not mutate calibration state.
_Novelty:_ Locks the engineering posture to prevent over-building for a multi-deployment world we don't yet inhabit. Avoids the classic trap of optimizing for statistics before the statistics exist.

**[SCOPE #3] Core Product Surfaces — Day-to-Day Actionability, Visibility, Phase & Task Tracking**
_Concept:_ The V1 product has four primary surfaces. Every other capability is secondary and deferrable.
_The four surfaces:_
1. **Everyday Actionable Surfacing** — Oracle's morning digest (3-item, ranked, cited per [RP-ORACLE #1]) and in-meeting alert ([RP-ORACLE #7–11]). This is what the strategist sees every day to know what matters *today*.
2. **Deployment Visibility / Timeline View** — a visualization of the account's growth: phases traversed, key events, identity graph evolution, learning landmarks. Serves the user's explicit ask: *"communicating and visualizing the timeline of an account's growth with insights."*
3. **Phase Tracking** — unambiguous, at-a-glance indication of which deployment phase the account is in, what transition signals are accruing, and what the next phase's prerequisites are.
4. **Task-at-Phase Tracking** — for the current phase, the set of tasks/actions that are necessary or high-leverage, pulled from the action queue ([FP #14]) and filtered by phase. Answers *"what do I need to be doing right now at this stage?"*
_What V1 deprioritizes (still supported in schema, not primary UI):_ sophisticated multi-agent negotiation traces, cross-deployment statistical dashboards, web-aware verification as a standing surface, calibration-review tooling beyond a basic audit log, blocker agent as a separate UI (blockers surface inside action-queue tasks).
_Novelty:_ Names the product. Four surfaces, not twenty. Every design trade-off gets weighed against whether it makes these four better or worse.

**[SCOPE #4] Don't Over-Engineer — Real Data Will Dictate the Real Design**
_Concept:_ Many design choices made during brainstorm (latency budgets, confidence-tier thresholds, ranker objective weights, calibration policies, phase-transition detectors) are **educated provisional defaults**. Real deployment data will teach the right values. The system's architecture must remain **simple enough to change** rather than clever enough to survive first contact unchanged.
_Practical discipline:_ (a) Prefer one working implementation over three configurable ones. (b) Prefer a dumb threshold with a calibration log ([ENG #3]) over a trained model. (c) Prefer a working UI over an elegant abstraction. (d) Any design choice justified purely by "it's elegant" or "it could scale to N customers" without a real user need from this deployment is out. (e) Defer anything that doesn't make one of the [SCOPE #3] four surfaces meaningfully better in the first real deployment.
_Novelty:_ An explicit counter-weight to the brainstorming impulse. Many of the atoms in this session are sophisticated; [SCOPE #4] is the reminder that sophistication is a liability until real data proves its necessity.

**[SCOPE #5] Documentation-as-Artifact — Logic & Thinking Are the Pre-Sale Deliverable**
_Concept:_ The **documentation is part of the product**, not a trailing artifact. It is sent to prospective customers as a **pre-visualization** of what they will receive, and the **logic and thinking** captured in it is itself a primary source of value — the product sells the thought process of dissecting a deployment as much as the software that executes it.
_Required documentation surfaces (maintained as living artifacts alongside code):_

1. **Agent Logic Docs** — for each agent (Cartographer, Oracle, Master Strategist), a plain-English document that explains: what the agent watches for, how it decides what to surface, what it deliberately does not do, how it expresses confidence, how it learns over time. Each agent doc traces back to specific atoms in this brainstorming session.
2. **Product Surface Docs** — for each of the four [SCOPE #3] surfaces (morning digest, in-meeting alert, timeline/visibility, phase & task tracking), a walkthrough of what the strategist sees, when, why, and how they interact with it.
3. **Deployment Logic Walkthrough** — a document that walks a customer through the seven phases of a deployment, showing for each phase: the typical signals, the typical stakeholders, the typical risks, the typical value-creation moments, and how the system supports the strategist at each stage. **This is the highest-leverage document** — it communicates the thinking about deployments that is the product's actual value.
4. **Scope Manifest** — explicit, side-by-side articulation of: (a) what is built and demo-ready today, (b) what is scoped and architected but not yet engineered, (c) what is explicitly out of scope (Not-To-Build per [SCOPE #1]), (d) what will be tuned once real deployment data flows (per [SCOPE #4]).
5. **Data & Ingestion Doc** — what the system consumes (email, voice, meetings, calendar, notes, task system), how it turns that into events and identity graph, and what data the customer will need to connect.
6. **Documentation Provenance Trail** — each major design decision in the docs traces back to the atoms in this brainstorming corpus (e.g., *"Oracle does not script meeting language — see [RP-ORACLE #5]"*). Gives customers a view into the rigor of the reasoning.

_Discipline:_ (a) Every atom committed in this brainstorm must have a traceable mapping to at least one documentation surface. (b) Documentation is updated when atoms are refined (per [ENG #3] calibration events). (c) Documentation is tested for accuracy against actual system behavior — *"the docs say morning digest shows 3 items; the code produces 3 items"* is a schema test per [ENG #2].
_Novelty:_ Positions documentation as a **sales surface and a design artifact**, not an afterthought. Creates a tight loop: brainstorming atoms → docs → code → tests → back to atoms when reality teaches something new. Customers get the thinking as a deliverable, which is itself a differentiator.

**[SCOPE #1] Not-To-Build — Deployment Strategist Tool, Not Competitive Intelligence**
_Concept:_ The system's scope is bounded to **(a) the deployment strategist's day-to-day success inside an active government account** and **(b) communicating and visualizing the timeline of that account's growth with surfaced insights.** It is explicitly **not**: a competitive-threat awareness system, a market-intelligence mapper, an outside-vendor tracker, or a counter-positioning tool. Signals about the outside world are handled by a different system (out of this product's surface).
_Implication:_ Events/relations are only meaningful insofar as they live **inside the deployment's interaction surface** (our people ↔ customer's people ↔ customer's embedded consultants/vendors, as stakeholders within the account's operating context). "Urban Structures sent the DC a deck" is in-scope only as **stakeholder-attention-drift** (the DC's advisory inputs are shifting), not as competitive event. The system does not open a "competitive arc," does not draft counter-narratives, does not track outside firms' positioning.
_Novelty:_ Locks the Not-To-Build line early so future atoms don't drift into surveillance / moat-defense tooling. The product is for **the human running the deployment**, not for the business protecting itself from competitors. Anti-feature surface prevents agent design from bloating into all-of-sales.

**[RP-ORACLE #1] Three-Item Hard Budget for the Morning Digest**
_Concept:_ The morning digest is capped at **3 items**. Scannability is a product property. The cap forces the ranker to actually prioritize rather than dump a feed; it forces Oracle to *decide* what matters most.
_Novelty:_ Ranking pressure is a design primitive, not a UX detail. A 3-item cap is what separates a strategist tool from a notifications feed.

**[RP-ORACLE #2] "What I Ranked Out" Transparency Footer**
_Concept:_ Below the 3 surfaced items, Oracle shows 2–3 items it considered and deliberately ranked out, each with a one-line reason. Clicking any ranked-out item promotes it back into the digest and emits a `ranking_override` event.
_Novelty:_ Makes Oracle's ranking function legible to the strategist, prevents the "why didn't you tell me?" failure mode, and creates a cheap calibration loop — overrides become learning signal for Oracle's ranker.

**[RP-ORACLE #3] Corpus-Confidence Marker on Cross-Deployment Learnings**
_Concept:_ Every learning pulled from the cross-deployment library carries a **corpus-confidence tier** separate from its evidence tier. A learning backed by 3 prior deployments is *provisional-across-corpus*; a learning backed by 30 is *strong-across-corpus*. Oracle never presents cross-corpus learnings as if the corpus were unlimited.
_Novelty:_ Separates the two confidence axes (evidence within a deployment vs. statistical weight across deployments) that most RAG systems conflate. Matches [FP #5.l] tenancy-aware projection.

**[RP-ORACLE #4] Null-Result Retrieval is a First-Class Output**
_Concept:_ Oracle can surface the *absence* of an expected event as a finding ("the Efficiency Report hasn't been delivered yet"). Learnings in the library can specify an expected deliverable type at a given phase; Oracle queries the deployment log for its presence and surfaces the negative match. Ties to [FP #5.j] silence-as-event.
_Novelty:_ Most retrieval systems only return what exists. A deployment tool must return what's conspicuously missing — that's often the highest-leverage insight.

**[RP-ORACLE #5] Oracle Surfaces Evidence and Named Patterns, Not Prescriptive Language**
_Concept:_ Oracle may surface **named alternative patterns** drawn from a solidified learning's evidence snapshot — e.g., *"a 3-day Minimum Viable Calibration has been accepted in 2 prior cases."* Oracle does **not** draft sentences, propose phrasing, or tell the strategist what to say. The distinction: surfacing a pattern **backed by prior evidence** (allowed) vs. generating **prescriptive language** (not allowed).
_Constraints:_ (a) Any surfaced pattern must resolve to a specific entry in a solidified learning's evidence snapshot — retrieval-bound per [ENG #1], never generated on the fly. (b) Framing is always conditional: *"consider X"*, *"pattern Y has worked in N/N prior cases"* — never *"push back on Z"* or *"say ..."* (c) The strategist is the sole author of meeting language; the pattern is a cited handle, not a script.
_Novelty:_ Moves the line from pure information-surface to information-surface + retrieval-bound pattern suggestion. Respects [FP #19] senior-strategist mindset while adding real leverage — the strategist gets a named, cited path they can choose to use, reshape, or ignore. The safety margin is that patterns are retrieved (real, citable) not generated (hallucination risk in live setting).

**[RP-ORACLE #6] Leverage-at-This-Moment Ranking**
_Concept:_ Oracle's ranking objective is *"what can the strategist move from provisional → strong, or from blocked → unblocked, in the next N minutes of the next interaction?"* — not topical similarity, not recency, not volume. The ranker is phase-aware, meeting-aware, and action-queue-aware.
_Novelty:_ Reframes retrieval as leverage optimization. Directly operationalizes the "temporal relevance" problem the user flagged at session open.

**[RP-ORACLE #7] In-Meeting Mode: Retrieval-Only, Solidified-Only, Private-Only**
_Concept:_ Three hard constraints make live in-meeting alerts safe. (a) **Retrieval-only** — Oracle surfaces existing library entries; it does not synthesize new claims while a meeting is live. (b) **Solidified-only** — draft/provisional learnings never trigger in-meeting alerts; the floor is at least `solidified`. (c) **Private-only** — alerts appear only on the strategist's private surface; never audible, never visible to the customer, never on-screen in shared views.
_Novelty:_ Live-setting risk envelope drawn explicitly. In-meeting is not where the system gets creative; it is where it is most conservative.

**[RP-ORACLE #8] Learning-Card Never Paraphrases the Trigger**
_Concept:_ The in-meeting alert card references the **learning** (L-031, its evidence, its phase match), not the utterance that triggered it. The card says "skipping on-site calibration → re-scan event (2/2 prior)" — not "the customer said 'let's skip calibration.'"
_Novelty:_ Decouples alert content from ASR quality and from semantic-interpretation risk. If the transcriber mis-hears or the trigger matcher over-fires, the card is still grounded in a real, cited learning. Eliminates a whole class of hallucination surfaces.

**[RP-ORACLE #9] Phase-Tuned Confidence Floors**
_Concept:_ The confidence floor that gates in-meeting alerts varies by phase. Phase 4 (live training), Phase 5 (value creation), and Phase 6 (expansion prep) have the highest floors because interrupt cost is high and the customer is engaged. Phase 1 (discovery) has a lower floor — the strategist has more freedom to surface tentative patterns.
_Novelty:_ Recognizes that *when* a learning fires matters as much as *whether* it fires. Calibrated per phase; subject to tuning per [ENG #3].

**[RP-ORACLE #10] Alert-Disposition as First-Class Post-Meeting Event**
_Concept:_ Every in-meeting alert receives a post-meeting **disposition event**: `handled`, `false_positive`, `unaddressed`, or `implicit` (topic resolved in transcript without the strategist tapping). Dispositions feed the ranker, adjust the learning's confidence trajectory, and calibrate future thresholds per [ENG #3]. `unaddressed` dispositions can promote to action-queue candidates.
_Novelty:_ Closes the loop from alert → outcome → calibration. Without this, alerts are a monologue; with it, they are the feedback signal that makes Oracle actually get better.

**[RP-ORACLE #11] Latency Budget as a Hard Gate**
_Concept:_ If Oracle can't surface an in-meeting alert within ~8 seconds of the trigger, it does not surface in-meeting at all — the finding is rerouted to evening synthesis. Stale in-meeting alerts are worse than no alert. (The 8-second default is a provisional calibration per [ENG #3], will tune with disposition data.)
_Novelty:_ Bounds intrusiveness by time, not just by confidence. A truth that arrives 2 minutes after the strategist moved on is a distraction, not a help.

**[RP-ORACLE #12] Evening Synthesis is the Only Mode Where New-Learning Candidates Are Proposed**
_Concept:_ Morning digest retrieves. In-meeting retrieves. **Only evening synthesis proposes new candidates.** Three distinct modes with distinct risk profiles. Keeps the riskiest operation (new claim generation) in the least time-pressured mode where evidence can be fully assembled and human review is frictionless.
_Novelty:_ A temporal partitioning of agent capability by risk. Prevents in-meeting claim generation (highest hallucination risk) while still letting the system produce new knowledge daily.

**[RP-ORACLE #13] Tiered Solidification — Auto-Enter for Most, Gate for High-Impact**
_Concept:_ Most learning candidates **auto-enter** the library as `provisional-in-deployment` with a lightweight **weekly batch review** where the strategist can retroactively edit/reject. A per-candidate approve/edit/reject gate is required **only** for high-impact candidates: (a) cross-deployment promotion candidates (moving from in-deployment to across-corpus), (b) candidates eligible to trigger in-meeting alerts (high false-positive cost per [RP-ORACLE #9]), (c) candidates that contradict an existing solidified learning, (d) candidates about identity/role changes that cascade to multiple downstream systems.
_Novelty:_ Gate overhead matches blast radius. Strategist time is not spent rubber-stamping low-stakes within-deployment observations; it is concentrated on the candidates whose errors actually propagate.

**[RP-ORACLE #14] Rejection with Reason is a First-Class Signal**
_Concept:_ When the strategist rejects a candidate (either at the gate or in weekly batch review), the reason is captured as **structured signal** (false positive / already-known / wrong phase / wrong customer-type / contradicts-L-XXX / other), never just a boolean. The reason feeds [ENG #3] calibration and [FP #11.c] rejection-as-signal.
_Novelty:_ Rejection data is the highest-quality calibration fuel. Throwing it away as a boolean wastes it.

**[RP-ORACLE #15] Disposition Follow-Through — Oracle Closes Its Own Loops**
_Concept:_ In-meeting alerts don't disappear after a meeting. Each one resurfaces in evening synthesis with a **disposition gate** (`handled` / `false_positive` / `unaddressed` / `implicit`) so the strategist can confirm resolution or promote to a follow-up action. No alert drops on the floor.
_Novelty:_ Closes the loop from alert → meeting outcome → disposition → ranker calibration. Without this, the in-meeting mode is a one-way broadcast; with it, the system's behavior measurably improves with use.

**[RP-MS #1] Master Strategist as Integration Layer, Not a UI Surface**
_Concept:_ The Master Strategist is **not a separate dashboard** in V1. It is the **synthesis layer** whose outputs feed the four [SCOPE #3] surfaces. Its jobs: (a) rank the action queue and learning-surface candidates under the [FP #13] dual-objective stack (phase objective + North Star), (b) arbitrate [FP #12] negotiations when Cartographer and Oracle disagree, (c) escalate to the User Validation Queue when confidence falls below calibrated floors, (d) compute "leverage-at-this-moment" scores per [RP-ORACLE #6] that drive morning-digest and action-queue ordering.
_Inputs consumed:_ Cartographer's identity/relation graph, Oracle's retrieval outputs, event log, learning library, action queue state, User Validation Queue state, current phase and phase-transition signals.
_Outputs produced:_ ranked action queue, ranked morning digest, escalation events, negotiation resolutions, phase-transition proposals.
_Novelty:_ Names the integration layer explicitly so it doesn't become a UI-sprawl temptation. The strategist experiences Master Strategist indirectly through the four surfaces, not through a "priority dashboard" of its own. Aligns with [SCOPE #3] four-surface discipline.

**[RP-MS #2] Phase Transitions Are Agent-Proposed, Human-Confirmed**
_Concept:_ Phase transitions (e.g., Phase 5 → Phase 6) are detected by Master Strategist from **structured signals** — presence of phase-exit deliverables in the event log, appearance of phase-entry stakeholder types in the identity graph, meeting-agenda content matching phase-entry patterns, strategist's own action-queue completion state. Detection produces a **transition proposal** in the User Validation Queue, not an automatic transition. Strategist confirms, rejects, or edits.
_Novelty:_ Phase state is too consequential to let an agent change silently (downstream Oracle retrievals, Cartographer tier thresholds, and action-queue filters all rekey on it). Also too useful to make fully manual. The middle path: agent detects and proposes with citation envelope; human gates.

**[RP-BLOCKER #1] Blockers Are a Typed Event Class, Not a Separate Agent in V1**
_Concept:_ Blockers are detected by Cartographer via [FP #18] Cross-Modal Discernment as a **typed event category** (`blocker_candidate`). They do not get a standalone dashboard or agent in V1. Instead: (a) each detected blocker becomes an action-queue item with type `blocker_removal`, (b) they filter naturally into the phase-filtered task view per [SCOPE #3.4], (c) Oracle can retrieve prior blocker-removal patterns as cited evidence (per [RP-ORACLE #5]).
_V1 posture:_ typed detection, no new surface. If real-deployment data later justifies a standalone Blocker view, that's a post-V1 decision per [SCOPE #2] and [SCOPE #4].
_Novelty:_ Resists the temptation to spawn an agent per capability. Blocker-handling is a behavior of the shared surfaces, not a new product area.

**[RP-CART #9] Person as Stable Identity with Evolving Attribute History**
_Concept:_ A person is modeled as **one persistent entity node**. Role, AdvocacyScore, committee memberships, reporting relationships, and title are all **time-versioned attributes** on that node. A role change never creates a new entity — it appends a new version to the role attribute's history. Maria-the-Director and Maria-the-Transitioning are always the same Maria.
_Novelty:_ Crystallizes what [FP #16.f] and [FP #17] implied: AdvocacyScore(t) is a real time series on a single identity, not a broken trail across duplicate nodes. Makes phase-to-phase belief comparisons ("who did we think Maria was in Phase 2 vs. Phase 5?") a native query, not a join-across-duplicates exercise.

**Corrected Scene 2 (Option A) — deltas after senior-strategist pass:**

- **Confidence as tier labels, not numbers.** Move from 0.68 / 0.85 to: `draft / provisional / strong / authoritative`. Each tier has defined ladder rules (per [FP #16.c]) and routes differently: `authoritative` auto-commits, `strong` commits with a notification, `provisional` enters the User Validation Queue, `draft` stays in the extraction review digest until it reaches provisional.
- **Web-aware verification runs before role-change proposals.** Per [RP-CART #7], the web-aware sub-agent checks NYC DOT press releases, NYC.gov staff pages, and LinkedIn before a role-change candidate leaves draft tier. Its findings enter as corroborating events with source attribution. For John Washington, external corroboration could raise the proposal from `strong` to `authoritative`; for Maria, the *absence* of an announcement is itself signal and stays `provisional`.
- **Brief what we know before proposing.** Before emitting a role-change proposal, the Cartographer produces a one-screen summary of all prior interactions with that identity (per [RP-CART #9], all tied to the single node). "Here is what we know about John Washington. Now here is the proposed change." Prevents jumping to proposal without context.
- **Propose one action, don't ask users to pick.** Q3 replaced with: *"I'll hold Maria's AdvocacyScore at 0.72 with a 14-day re-examination task. Override if you want a different default."* Single default with one-click override beats multi-choice questions for busy Strategists.
- **Auto-cue the Master Strategist for AdvocacyScore revisits.** A detected role-change automatically opens a negotiation with the Master Strategist proposing a specific action ("schedule a 15-min with John in the next 10 days") rather than the Cartographer silently waiting for the Strategist to notice.

## MVP Demo Definition — What Must Work for a First-Customer Pre-Visualization

The MVP is scoped against a single goal: **a live, seeded-data demo that a prospective customer (e.g., NYC DOT) can walk through and immediately understand how the system will support them on a real deployment**, backed by documentation rigorous enough to be sent as a pre-visualization before any contract is signed.

### Demo-Ready Capabilities (Must Work on Seeded Data)

**A. Data Ingestion (stubs acceptable where connectors aren't built)**
- Email ingestion → events in the log, parties into the identity graph.
- Meeting recordings → transcription → events with speaker attribution.
- Calendar sync → meeting events with attendees pre-linked to identities.
- Manual notes / field notes upload → events with attestation per [FP #5.h].
- Task system (Linear) → action-queue mirroring (one-way read in V1 is fine).

**B. Canonical Memory (per [ENG #1])**
- Identity graph: person, organization, committee, role entities, with time-versioned attributes per [RP-CART #9].
- Event log: all ingestion paths produce typed, attributed, time-stamped events per [FP #5.a–5.h].
- Learning library: seeded with 5–10 solidified learnings from synthetic "prior municipal deployments," each with evidence snapshot per [FP #5.i], phase tags, corpus-confidence markers per [RP-ORACLE #3].
- Action queue: populated from extraction + learnings + dispositions, filterable by phase and priority.
- User Validation Queue: populated from Cartographer/Oracle uncertainty.

**C. The Four Surfaces (per [SCOPE #3], every one must demo)**
1. **Morning Digest** — 3-item, ranked, cited, with "ranked out" footer. Demonstrate on a seeded "Day 47 of deployment" scenario with a live simulated 10am DC meeting on the calendar.
2. **In-Meeting Alert** — demonstrate live during a scripted demo call (or playback). Show the alert card with learning citation, named-pattern surfacing per [RP-ORACLE #5], and disposition capture.
3. **Timeline / Visibility View** — a visualization of the account's growth: phases traversed, key events plotted, identity graph evolution, learning landmarks. This is the single most persuasive visual surface for prospective customers; it makes the "Living Digital Twin" tangible.
4. **Phase & Task Tracking** — at-a-glance current phase, transition signals accruing, phase-specific tasks, and *"what you need to be doing right now."*

**D. Agent Runtimes**
- Cartographer: passive entity/relation extraction from seeded communications; role-change detection with tier labels per the Corrected Scene 2 deltas; User Validation Queue population.
- Oracle: phase-gated retrieval for morning digest + in-meeting alerts; evening synthesis producing tiered candidates per [RP-ORACLE #13].
- Master Strategist: ranking and arbitration as an integration layer per [RP-MS #1]; phase-transition proposals per [RP-MS #2]. No dedicated UI.

**E. Phase-State Machine**
- All 7 phases defined as typed states.
- Phase-transition detectors running (even if simple threshold rules to start, per [SCOPE #4]).
- Phase-gated retrieval verified in all four surfaces.

**F. Documentation Package (per [SCOPE #5], shippable alongside code)**
- Agent Logic Docs (3 — one each for Cartographer, Oracle, Master Strategist).
- Product Surface Docs (4 — one per surface in [SCOPE #3]).
- **Deployment Logic Walkthrough** — the highest-leverage doc; walks through 7 phases showing how the system supports the strategist.
- Scope Manifest — built / scoped-but-not-built / out-of-scope / tune-on-real-data.
- Data & Ingestion Doc.
- Documentation Provenance Trail — docs traceable back to atoms in this brainstorm.

**G. Testing Readiness (per [ENG #2])**
- All deterministic code paths covered by unit tests.
- Schema contract tests on every agent's I/O.
- Replay tests on the seeded demo scenario — the demo's digests, alerts, and syntheses are pinned to known-good outputs.
- Canonical-store invariant tests.

### Explicitly Out of MVP (Architected but Not Engineered)

- Live multi-agent negotiation trace UI.
- Cross-deployment analytics dashboard (only one real deployment will exist).
- Standalone Blocker agent UI per [RP-BLOCKER #1].
- Web-aware verification as a standing surface (used narrowly for identity disambiguation when needed).
- Calibration review UI beyond a basic audit log per [ENG #3].
- Advanced User Validation Queue workflows (simple queue UI only in V1).
- Cross-corpus statistical promotion machinery per [SCOPE #2].

### Demo Failure Modes to Pre-Empt (Anti-Solution, folded in from would-be Phase 3)

- **Demo flop — agent says something confidently wrong.** Guarded by [ENG #1] citation-envelope requirement + [RP-ORACLE #5] surfaces-not-scripts. Every surfaced fact must trace to a specific seeded event.
- **Demo flop — customer can't see themselves in the seeded data.** Mitigation: seed data that reflects a municipal-deployment archetype close enough to NYC DOT's reality that the customer recognizes the shape.
- **Documentation oversell — docs promise behavior the code doesn't match.** Guarded by schema tests per [ENG #2] that assert doc claims against code behavior; any mismatch fails the test.
- **Onboarding flop — customer's first real data floods or breaks the system.** Mitigation: schema tolerance for partial / messy inputs; visible User Validation Queue for ambiguous cases per [RP-CART #8] symbiotic posture.
- **First-deployment calibration surprise — seeded thresholds wildly wrong for reality.** Mitigation: [ENG #3] calibration history makes early reality-check adjustments visible and reversible; [SCOPE #4] expects this and has built the system to absorb it.

## Key Design Commitments (Non-Negotiable)

These are the decisions downstream PRD / architecture work inherits as fixed. Changing any of them requires an explicit revisit of the brainstorm.

1. **Canonical memory is the single source of truth.** Every agent output carries a citation envelope. [ENG #1]
2. **100% test coverage mandate with replay tests.** Every deterministic path + every agent I/O contract. [ENG #2]
3. **Adaptive logic via audited, reversible calibration.** No silent model drift; every parameter change is a logged event. [ENG #3]
4. **Four product surfaces, not twenty.** Morning digest, in-meeting alert, timeline/visibility, phase & task tracking. [SCOPE #3]
5. **First-deployment-first engineering posture.** Cross-corpus machinery architected, not operationally engineered. [SCOPE #2]
6. **Deployment strategist tool, not competitive intelligence.** External-threat awareness is out of scope forever. [SCOPE #1]
7. **Documentation is part of the product.** Agent logic docs, surface docs, deployment logic walkthrough, scope manifest, ingestion docs — all shipped alongside code. [SCOPE #5]
8. **Person = stable identity with time-versioned attributes.** No duplicate-identity fragmentation on role change. [RP-CART #9]
9. **Surfaces, doesn't script.** Oracle surfaces facts, learnings, absences, and named cited patterns; the strategist owns meeting language. [RP-ORACLE #5]
10. **Retrieval-only in-meeting; generation only in evening synthesis with human gate on high-impact candidates.** [RP-ORACLE #7, #12, #13]
11. **Master Strategist is an integration layer, not a UI surface.** [RP-MS #1]
12. **Phase transitions are agent-proposed, human-confirmed.** [RP-MS #2]
13. **Symbiotic posture.** Agent surfaces for human; above-threshold auto-commits, below-threshold queues for validation. [RP-CART #8]
14. **Don't over-engineer; real data dictates real design.** Every design trade-off weighed against first-deployment reality. [SCOPE #4]

## Open Questions for Real Deployment Data

Deliberately deferred; to be answered with the first real deployment rather than over-specified now.

1. **Latency budget for in-meeting alerts.** Provisional ~8s per [RP-ORACLE #11]; real strategist glance-frequency will tune this.
2. **Phase-tuned confidence floors.** Floor values per phase are educated defaults; first-deployment false-positive/false-negative rates will calibrate.
3. **Tier-promotion thresholds.** Provisional → solidified promotion rules need real-deployment base-rates to set sensibly.
4. **Weekly batch review cadence.** Weekly is a default; actual strategist appetite/capacity may push it to bi-weekly or daily-light.
5. **Ranker objective weights.** Leverage-at-this-moment is a principle [RP-ORACLE #6]; the exact weighting of phase-objective vs North-Star vs recency needs empirical tuning.
6. **Silence calibration defaults per identity type.** Per-identity, OOO-aware thresholds [FP #5.k] will be sharpened with real absence data.
7. **Phase-transition signal rules.** V1 uses simple threshold rules per [SCOPE #4]; real transitions will reveal the nuanced signals.
8. **Extraction review digest volume.** How much Cartographer pushes into the digest vs auto-commits vs queues is tunable and will be over-corrected in both directions before settling.
9. **Documentation freshness cadence.** How often logic docs re-sync with code changes as atoms are refined post-launch.
10. **Seed data realism sufficiency for NYC DOT demo.** Recognizing whether the seeded demo resonates with the prospective customer's reality is itself an open question until the first demo lands.
